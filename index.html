◆ THE THESIS ◆

We don't need to find the next Babe Ruth.
We just need a lineup that gets on base.

The Problem

Power-Law VC is broken for the middle market.

The traditional venture model bets nine times out of ten on strikeouts to fund one moonshot home run. It works for the top decile of firms — and almost nobody else. Founders burn out chasing $1B narratives. LPs wait a decade for outcomes that may never come.

Meanwhile, the most reliable wealth in America is built by companies sold for $10–50M — businesses that quietly throw singles, doubles, and the occasional triple. That's the inefficiency we're attacking.

★ THE MATH 40 portfolio companies × ~58% reach a ~$25M exit at 30% ownership ≈ ~$174M returned on a ~$19M fund — roughly a 9.0x gross multiple, or a ~900% fund return, before fees and carry.

◆ THE PLAYBOOK ◆

From scouting to a strategic exit.

A six-inning game plan. Every Small Ball company runs the same disciplined sequence.

01

Scout the Field

We identify a $50M–$500M sub-vertical with regulatory friction, sleepy incumbents, or an obvious distribution chokepoint. We look for singles, not moonshots.

02

Sign the JV Partner First

Before recruiting a founder, we lock a field expert or strategic JV partner. They commit pilots, contracts, or co-sell capacity. No JV, no company.

03

Draft the Founder

We recruit a scrappy, operator-grade founder — typically a 2-to-3-time builder — who we pair with the JV partner as co-pilot. Equity is split to align outcomes.

04

Incubate in the Studio

6–12 months of in-house ops support: legal, finance, product, design, hiring. We stop being involved when the team can run the bases without us.

05

Get on Base

Hit revenue milestones. Sign a second customer outside the JV. Build a defensible wedge inside the sub-vertical. Singles compound.

06

Engineer the Exit

3–5 years in, we run a structured M&A process — often with the JV partner as a natural acquirer. The ~$25M exit was always the plan, not a fallback.
